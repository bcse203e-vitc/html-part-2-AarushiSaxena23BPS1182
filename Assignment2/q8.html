Welcome to Cologne Cathedral

Explore the history and significance of the magnificent Cologne Cathedral, a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of Germany's architectural legacy.

The Cologne Cathedral (Kölner Dom) is a stunning Gothic masterpiece and one of the largest cathedrals in Europe. Located in the heart of Cologne, Germany, it has been a symbol of faith and perseverance for over 700 years. Construction began in 1248 but was not completed until 1880, with the cathedral’s iconic twin spires reaching a height of 157 meters, making it the tallest twin-spired church in the world. The cathedral is renowned for its intricate architectural details, including over 700 stained glass windows, some dating back to the 13th century, which flood the interior with colorful light. It houses the Shrine of the Three Kings, believed to hold the relics of the Magi, attracting millions of pilgrims and visitors annually. The Cologne Cathedral is not just an architectural marvel but also a UNESCO World Heritage site, standing as a symbol of the city’s resilience and religious heritage. Its grandeur and historical significance make it one of Germany’s most important landmarks.
