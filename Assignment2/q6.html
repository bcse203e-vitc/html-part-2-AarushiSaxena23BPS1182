Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Home| Recipes| Tips

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add pancetta and cook until crispy.
  3. Beat eggs in a bowl and mix with grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.
  4. Once the pasta is ready, drain it and reserve a bit of pasta water.
  5. Add the hot pasta to the pan with pancetta and garlic. Toss to coat.
  6. Remove the pan from heat and quickly stir in the egg and Parmesan mixture, adding reserved pasta water to create a creamy sauce.
  7. Serve with extra Parmesan and freshly ground black pepper.

Nutritional Information

  • Calories: 450 kcal per serving
  • Protein: 20g
  • Carbohydrates: 50g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Fiber: 3g

Fun Fact

Did you know that Carbonara is believed to have originated in Rome in the 1940s? Its creamy texture comes from eggs and cheese, not cream!
